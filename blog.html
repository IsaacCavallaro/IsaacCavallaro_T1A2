Audio Collage Experiment



April, 2021

During my time subletting in NYC in 2017, I set myself a challenge to produce an idea a day. The objective was to primarily use sampled audio (including sounds I sourced from around the city) and to throw them together in a kind of “audio collage”. The challenge was to only allow one day to collect the sounds, edit them and then walk away.

Augmented Drumming



April, 2021

In 2019 I commenced my music research at Griffith University. My project examines the integration of the MIDI keyboard alongside the Traditional Drumset (TD) and suggests that the continual renewal of technology has both shaped the TD and led to the integration of digital technologies. Using a practice-led research model along with action research, my research project offers TD practitioners new tools for developing a solo percussion practice using what I call the Augmented Drumset (AD). The primary aim was to develop an idiolect for the AD; achieved through playing, practising, and composing for the AD, devising six parameters, resulting in an iterative process termed the Augmented Drumset Iterative Cycle (ADIC).
